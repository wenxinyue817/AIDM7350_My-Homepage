My Project

Study on the Dissemination Effect of Mainstream Media's Short Videos under the Background of Media Integration

The project summarizes the mainstream media's short video dissemination mode based on the 5W communication model and analyzes the dissemination elements in detail. Using SPSS, the project also constructs a measurement index system for short video transmission power, with three primary indicators and six secondary indicators.
This is followed by empirical research through case analysis and in-depth interviews exploring the dissemination effect of mainstream media's short videos. In particular, the "CCTV News" TikTok account is selected as the case study object, with quantitative and qualitative data collected on factors like likes, comments, shares, and new and total followers. Findings show that mainstream media's short videos have strong network transmission power, although there are concerns over content homogenization, entertainment, and catering to the audience. The project offers theoretical and practical references to help optimize the production of distinct, professionalized short videos in overcoming these issues.

Study on the Media Representation and Evolution of Women's Issues on Weibo in the Past Decade

The project analyzed textual data from the "Sina Women" Weibo account over the past decade, using text analysis techniques to explore the popularity, themes, and emotional evolution of women's issues on the platform.
The study utilized Python to conduct LDA topic analysis, DTM topic temporal evolution analysis, and snowNLP sentiment analysis, and presented the findings through visual analysis in Gephi. The research found that over the past ten years, the popularity of women's issues on Weibo has shown a trend of low-high-low, with an increasing richness of themes and a gradual shift towards negative sentiment.
Based on these conclusions, the project analyzed the causes of this evolution from the perspectives of politics and economy, mass media, and social public opinion, explored the problems inherent in the representation of women's issues in the media, and proposed corresponding optimization recommendations in order to bridge the gender divide and conflicts present in current online discussions of women's issues and promote more rational discourse.
